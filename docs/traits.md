# Character Traits — Player Guide

Every monarch and noble in your dynasty carries **personality traits**. Traits are
not just flavor: they apply concrete modifiers to combat, taxation, and diplomacy,
and some even shorten a character's life. The reigning monarch's traits are shown
in the tooltip of their portrait in the left rail of the world map, and in the
monarch detail card.

There are **8 traits**. Combat and tax effects are applied as multipliers
(e.g. `+15%` means the value is multiplied by `1.15`); diplomacy effects are flat
additive points. Multiple traits stack — their deltas are summed before being
applied.

---

## The 8 Traits

### Brave
A fearless leader who inspires soldiers on the battlefield.
- **Combat: +15%** — armies led by a Brave monarch hit harder.

### Craven
A timid ruler whose hesitation costs lives in war.
- **Combat: -15%** — armies led by a Craven monarch fight noticeably worse.

### Cunning
A shrewd schemer skilled in negotiation and intrigue.
- **Diplomacy: +15** — strongly improves diplomatic outcomes.

### Wroth
A hot-tempered ruler, quick to anger and quick to war.
- **Combat: +10%** — fury drives soldiers forward.
- **Diplomacy: -10** — temper undermines negotiations.

### Patient
A measured, even-tempered ruler who waits for the right moment.
- **Combat: +5%** — steady command yields a modest edge in battle.
- **Diplomacy: +10** — patience smooths negotiations.

### Pious
A devout ruler respected by clergy and faithful subjects.
- **Diplomacy: +10** — faith earns goodwill at the negotiating table.

### Greedy
An avaricious ruler who squeezes every coin from the realm.
- **Tax: +20%** — collects substantially more tax revenue.
- **Diplomacy: -5** — reputation for greed sours relations.

### Sickly
A frail ruler in poor health throughout their reign.
- **Combat: -5%** — weakness slightly weakens command in the field.
- **Lifespan: halved** — a Sickly character lives roughly half as long, so plan
  your succession early.

## Building Gates

Some military units cannot be recruited until you construct the right building in
a territory:

- **Stables** — required to recruit **cavalry**.
- **Barracks** — required to recruit **heavy infantry**.

Plan your construction before you plan your army.

---

## Trait Inheritance

When a child is born into the dynasty, they may inherit traits from their parents:

- Each parental trait has roughly a **30% chance** to pass to the child.
- A child can inherit at most **3 traits** from their parents.
- A child may additionally gain **one extra themed trait** appropriate to your
  dynasty's theme.

Over generations, breeding for strong traits (and marrying spouses who carry
them) can shape the character of your entire dynasty.
